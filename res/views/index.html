How to play

Imperium is a game for 2-4 players. Your objective is to move your king to the golden square in the middle. The players are assigned a random color, and take turns to make moves, following the order: Red, Green, Blue, Yellow.

There are three unity types: King, Thief and Warrior.

Movement

Warrior

Warriors move horizontally and vertically

Thief

Thieves move diagonally

King

Kings move like warriors

Capturing

You can capture wherever you can move

Terrain

You cannot move or attack into tree tiles.

Power Moves

Whenever one of your units (but not your king!) gets into the golden square, you can perform a power move. First, you select one enemy unity to destroy. You can't destroy kings, of course! Next, you select an allied unit and place a tree under it. This unit is now invulnerable to attacks, but can still attack. If you move, you cannot go back on top of the tree!

Each unit can only activate this power once! They gain a small white circle on the top left to show they can't activate it again.

Eliminating

If your king is killed, you are out of the game, and all your units are destroyed.

Winning

You win by moving your king into the golden saquare or by killing every enemy king.

Notation

While playing, the game will display the moves in this notation:

Normal moves: c3d2
Captures: c3d2!
Power moves: e4e5*a2$e3+ (move to, kill, create tree)
Power moves (with capture): e4e5!*a2$e3+ (capture, kill, create tree)
Elimination: c3d2!-
Win: e4e5#

This is the full board state notation: @a1:k1 @a2:t1 @b2:w1 @d3+ @e8+

The first three describe unit location: k = king, t = thief, w = warrior, followed by the unit's owner (1-based). Next is a list of trees.

Based on a Minecraft map by Pollieboy and Vilder50
